## A Fresh Perspective on Research

The seminar's theme revolved around decolonizing research methodologies. As a cybersecurity researcher, this concept was relatively new to me. The discussions opened my eyes to how cultural nuances deeply influence cybersecurity policies and practices. It was enlightening to see how different cultural dimensions, like social norms and individualism versus collectivism, play a crucial role in shaping online behavior and privacy concerns.

## The Power of Language and Communication

Participating in a predominantly English-speaking environment while coming from a francophone background presented its unique challenges and rewards. It was a test and a testament to my linguistic abilities, especially when Swahili came into play during fieldwork. This experience underscored the importance of language skills in bridging cultural gaps and enhancing research effectiveness.

## Special Encounters and Lasting Bonds

One of the most remarkable aspects of the seminar was the array of special meetings and connections I made. Each day brought new interactions with people from vastly different backgrounds, yet we found common ground in our shared passion for knowledge and understanding. These weren't just fleeting academic exchanges; they were the beginnings of what I believe will be lasting friendships and professional relationships.

I recall vivid conversations over coffee breaks, where ideas flowed as freely as the coffee itself. There were moments of profound insight during fieldwork, where the practical application of our discussions came to life. And then there were the evenings, filled with stories and laughter, reminding us that beyond our academic roles, we are all human, each with our unique journey.

These connections have enriched me both personally and professionally. They've expanded my network and opened doors to potential collaborations that I had never considered before. More importantly, they've gifted me with a global community of colleagues and friends, a network of support and inspiration that I will carry with me long after the seminar.

As I reflect on these encounters, I'm reminded of the power of human connection in the world of academia. It's not just about what we learn or research; it's about the people we meet along the way and the shared experiences that shape us.
