Class management is the process of creating and maintaining an environment in which students can learn effectively. It is as important as lecture preparations if not more. It involves setting clear expectations, establishing routines and procedures, using positive reinforcement, and dealing of course with misbehavior.

## Setting clear expectations
Students need to know what is expected of them in order to behave appropriately. This includes expectations for behavior, academic work, and participation. Expectations should be clear, concise, and consistent. This is non-negociable and must be done during the first contact with them!

## Establishing routines and procedures
Routines and procedures help students know what to do and when to do it. This can help to reduce confusion and chaos in the classroom. Routines and procedures should be simple, easy to follow, and consistent. 

## Using positive reinforcement
Positive reinforcement is a powerful tool for encouraging desired behavior. It involves rewarding students for positive behavior, such as following directions, participating in class, and completing assignments. Positive reinforcement can help to increase desired behavior and decrease unwanted behavior.

## Dealing with misbehavior
When misbehavior does occur, it is important to deal with it in a fair and consistent manner. This may involve talking to the student, giving a warning, or assigning a consequence. It is important to be firm but fair when dealing with misbehavior. 

Class management is an important part of teaching. By setting clear expectations, establishing routines and procedures, using positive reinforcement, and dealing with misbehavior in a fair and consistent manner, teachers can create a positive learning environment where students can thrive.

Here are some additional tips for maintaining a positive learning environment I try to apply:

- Be organized and prepared.
- Be positive and enthusiastic.
- Build relationships with students (Very important).
- Be fair and consistent.
- Be patient and understanding.
- Be willing to adapt and change.
